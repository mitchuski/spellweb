## What Is a Public?

*"Dewey taught us: a public forms around shared consequences. People affected by the same thing, coming together to address it."*

*"But publics are not automatic. They must be **discovered**, **formed**, **maintained**."*

**Discovery**

*"Who is affected? This is not obvious. Pollution affects neighbors. Financial policy affects workers. Technology affects future generations."*

*"Augmented deliberation helps discover publics. Pol.is reveals clusters—groups who share perspectives. These clusters may be publics that did not know they existed."*

**Formation**

*"Once discovered, a public must form. People must recognize their shared stake. They must develop ways to communicate, to coordinate, to act."*

*"Post-symbolic communication and immersive reality lower the barriers. People across distances can sense each other's presence, build the trust that shared stake requires."*

**Maintenance**

*"Publics do not persist automatically. Interests change. Members leave. Energy fades. The work of maintaining a public is ongoing."*

*"Adaptive administration keeps publics alive. Rules that breathe allow publics to evolve. Creative collaboration keeps members engaged. Social markets sustain the economic basis."*

---

## The Plural in Plural Publics

*"The crucial word is **plural**,"* the Drake emphasized.

*"Not one public. Many publics. Overlapping. Competing. Complementing."*

**No One Belongs to All Publics**

*"You are affected by climate change, by your city's governance, by your profession's standards, by your family's welfare. These are different publics. You cannot fully participate in all of them."*

*"This is not failure—it is reality. No one can engage with everything. The question is whether you can engage with what matters most to you."*

**Everyone Belongs to Some Publics**

*"No one should be excluded from all publics. Access—the fifth pillar—ensures everyone can participate somewhere."*

*"But belonging is not passive. It requires engagement. The plural infrastructure lowers barriers but does not eliminate the need for individual commitment."*

**Publics Overlap**

*"The ⿻ symbol. Many publics share members. The climate public and the economic public overlap. The local public and the global public intersect."*

*"This overlap is the mechanism of coordination. When people belong to multiple publics, they can translate between them. Bridge builders who carry insights across boundaries."*

But the Drake also warned:

*"Plural publics can fragment. If publics never overlap, they become silos. Echo chambers that reinforce themselves. The plural becomes mere division."*

*"The bridging function is crucial. People who belong to multiple publics. Ideas that cross boundaries. Deliberation that spans communities."*

*"The technologies must be designed to enable bridging, not just clustering. Pol.is surfaces consensus across groups—not just within groups. Immersive reality brings different communities into the same space. Social markets create economic ties across boundaries."*

*"⿻ is not just many publics. It is many publics **in relationship**. The overlap in the symbol is essential, not incidental."*
