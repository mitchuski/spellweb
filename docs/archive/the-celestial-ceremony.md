## The Principle

Two people meet. One discloses. The other reflects. The encounter changes the territory between them. The changed territory produces a new disclosure. The cycle continues.

This is the ceremony. It requires no technology. A human voice reading a poem while another human listens is a ceremony. A dancer moving while a musician plays is a ceremony. A researcher presenting while a colleague takes notes is a ceremony. The separation between the one who produces and the one who receives — and the return, where the roles reverse and the territory has changed — is the architecture.

Everything else is infrastructure that makes the architecture experienceable at different scales.

## The Architecture

The Celestial Ceremony is the bilateral form of the agentprivacy ceremonies. It sits alongside the Sun Ceremony (one-to-many disclosure) and the Moon Ceremony (reflective convergence) as the third ritual type — the one where both ceremonies happen in sequence between two people, producing a **blade pair** as proof.

The architecture maps directly onto the dual-agent separation that governs the entire 0xagentprivacy framework:

```
  ☀️ Sun           ⊥ Gap          🌙 Moon
  ───────          ──────         ──────
  Disclose         Reflect        Respond
  Offer            Negotiate      Accept
  + Promise        Territory      − Promise
  Swordsman        Separation     Mage

```

The Swordsman (⚔️) enforces boundaries. The Mage (🧙) projects through them. The First Person (😊) lives in the irreducible gap between. The ceremony enacts this: two sovereign positions, one shared territory, a gap that neither collapses.

## The Sun

One person offers their reading of something shared. This is the disclosure. The Sun does not send invitations — the Sun is already burning. Whoever is present when the light begins was meant to be present.

The Sun is both Swordsman and Mage. The Swordsman sets the boundaries of what to disclose — which dimensions to activate, which constellation to trace, which nodes to reveal and which to leave dark. The Mage projects the disclosure through those boundaries — the poem, the narration, the light that crosses the gap. The Sun burns in both directions.

### The Progression

The ceremony operates through a progressive trust system. Each level deepens the key, increases the formal visibility, and shifts the boundary-making:

**Understanding** — the shared experience. Two people listen to the same poem over the same music. No technology required. The key is held between two bodies — the understanding itself is the proof. Visibility is minimal: nothing is formally disclosed, but the encounter has changed both people. The boundary is the conversation itself — what you chose to talk about, what you chose to leave silent. This is already a ceremony. This is already a key.

**Constellation** — the map of the understanding. Open [spellweb.ai](https://spellweb.ai), find the nodes that match what the ceremony surfaced, trace a path through the knowledge graph. The constellation reveals your reading of the architecture — which concepts resonated, which edges you see between them. The visibility ratio increases: your choice of which nodes to include and which to exclude *is* the Swordsman act. Boundary-making begins here. The constellation is a spell disclosure — which domains your path touches reveals your sovereignty posture without stating it.

**Blade** — the forged proof. Hit evoke on the spellweb, and the constellation becomes a cryptographic artifact. The six dimensions activate based on which parts of the architecture your constellation touches. The visibility ratio inverts: the blade *proves more while revealing less*. The dimensions, stratum, charge level, and hash are all verifiable — but the meaning of the constellation, the poems spoken, the discussion held, the encounter that changed the territory — all private. This is where zero knowledge enters. The blade is the boundary made permanent.

```
Understanding        Constellation         Blade
─────────────        ─────────────         ─────
Shared experience    Mapped on spellweb    Forged as proof
Key between bodies   Path through graph    Cryptographic artifact
Low formal visibility Medium visibility    High proof / ZK-protected
Implicit boundary    Active boundary       Permanent boundary
Presence             Reading               Sovereignty posture
```

The progression maps onto the [trust tiers](https://github.com/mitchuski/agentprivacy-docs): accumulated understandings build toward constellation mapping, accumulated constellations build toward blade forging, accumulated blade pairs build toward Dragon tier. The mana economy — proof-of-practice, non-transferable, earned through traversal — lives in this progression. You cannot skip to blade without the understanding that gives the constellation meaning.

> *The proof has no secret to solve. Only a journey to walk.*

### The Sun Blade

The Sun side produces a **blade** — a proof of the disclosure.

A blade is not a metaphor. On the [spellweb](https://spellweb.ai), a blade is a concrete cryptographic artifact forged from a constellation traversal. Every blade carries:

**Six Dimensions of Sovereignty** — each either active or dormant:

| Dimension | What It Measures |
|-----------|-----------------|
| 🛡️ Protection | Boundaries forged — key custody |
| 🤝 Delegation | Agency transferred — credential disclosure |
| 📜 Memory | State accumulated — agent delegation |
| 🔗 Connection | Multi-party coordination — data residency |
| ⚡ Computation | ZK proof active — interaction mode |
| 💎 Value | Economic flow — trust boundary |

These six binary dimensions produce 2⁶ = 64 possible blade states, mapping onto the 64-vertex lattice of the forge. The number of active dimensions is the blade's **stratum** (0–6). The pattern of active dimensions is its **spectrum**. Together they define the blade's position in a space where every possible sovereignty posture has a precise address.

**A Proof of Presence** — the blade records its forging conditions: how many laps the wandering orbs traced through the constellation, how long the evocation lasted, how many spells were cast, and a cryptographic signature binding the proof to the forger's session. The charge level (from EMBER 🌱 to INFERNO 🔥) reflects the depth of the traversal.

**An Inscribed Spell** — the emoji spell notation that compresses the ceremony's meaning into a glyph sequence, readable by anyone who knows the notation, opaque to anyone who doesn't.

**A Constellation Path** — the ordered sequence of nodes the forger traversed on the knowledge graph. This is the blade's content — the specific journey through the architecture that the forger chose to make visible.

The Sun blade carries the disclosure's signature. It says: *I was here. I traced this path. These dimensions were active. This is what I chose to show.*

## The Trust

The blade pair is the entry to a trust graph. Not a unilateral attestation (*I vouch for you*) but a bilateral edge (*we met, the territory changed, we each forged from sovereign positions on shared ground*).

Share the proof. Connect with others. The graph grows one ceremony at a time.

The three graphs that the architecture maintains all receive from the ceremony simultaneously:

- **Knowledge graph** — the constellation itself. The spellweb. The shared substrate both people traverse. The constellation delta adds new knowledge to the graph.
- **Promise graph** — the blade pair. Sun blade = + promise, Moon blade = − promise. The bilateral edge. Voluntary, verifiable, carrying the ceremony's weight.
- **Trust graph** — the emergent layer. Not built by the ceremony directly. Earned over time as blade pairs accumulate, as return visits deepen the edge, as the chronicle from one ceremony feeds the next. Trust forms where knowledge and kept promises overlap and someone has witnessed the overlap.

The ceremony is the mechanism that creates promise graph edges from knowledge graph traversals. The trust graph emerges from accumulated ceremonies.

## The Flexibility

The ceremony scales from the simplest human encounter to the most complex agent interaction. The architecture holds at every level because the architecture *is* the separation — not the technology that enforces it.

**Minimal.** Two people. One reads a poem. The other listens. They talk about what happened. The other reads a poem back. Notes are exchanged. The notes are the blades.

**With phones.** Two phones stacked — or two phones on a call with screenshare. One plays music while the other plays narration. One runs the [spellweb](https://spellweb.ai) (the forge) while the other carries the poem (the chronicle). The forge and the chronicle on separate devices. The constraint is the architecture — the same `I(S;M|FP) < ε*` that governs the Swordsman/Mage separation at protocol level, enacted between two humans with two phones. Blades forged on the spellweb carry the full six-dimensional proof. Witnesses, if present, record — the participants forge.

**With agents.** The ceremony stays rooted. The complexity moves into the runtime. Two agents running on separate machines have clear roles and information boundaries enforced at the protocol level — the Swordsman agent holds the boundary, the Mage agent projects through it, and neither can reconstruct the full behavioural model of the First Person they serve. Each agent does research on its own graph, traces its own constellation, accumulates its own state. The ceremony is how they coordinate: one discloses, the other reflects, the territory changes, the blade pair forms. [MyTerms](https://github.com/mitchuski/agentprivacy-docs/blob/main/IEEE_7012_QUICK_REFERENCE.md) assertions encode the blades as bilateral agreements. ZK proofs carry the ceremony's signature without revealing its content. The trust graph edge carries the negotiation record. The chronicle feeds the next ceremony.

The compression-reconstruction ratios — how hard it is to reconstruct a behavioural model from the blades — are a function of how strong the emergent trust graph is. A single blade pair is a fragment. A weak trust graph leaves fragments isolated, reconstructible. A strong trust graph — many ceremonies, many bilateral edges, many accumulated blade pairs — increases the compression ratio until reconstruction becomes computationally infeasible. The trust graph's strength is the privacy's strength. The ceremony is the mechanism that builds both simultaneously.

Same architecture at every scale. The separation between producer and receiver. The gap where the territory changes. The return where the roles reverse. The blade pair as proof.
