# Chronicle: Master & Emissary Controls

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Feature:** Dual-Agent Input Mapping

---

## The Principle

The control scheme embodies the **Master & Emissary** relationship from McGilchrist's hemispheric model:

- **Right hemisphere (Master)** — holistic, spatial, deliberate
- **Left hemisphere (Emissary)** — sequential, verbal, reactive

The Swordsman is the Master. The Mage is the Emissary.

**Key insight:** The Swordsman *reveals* options. The Mage *executes* actions. They communicate through you.

## The Communication Flow

```
   ⚔️ Swordsman                    🧙 Mage
   (right-click)                  (left-click)
        │                              │
        │  "Here are my stances"       │
        ├─────────────────────────────>│
        │                              │
        │     You see the options      │
        │                              │
        │  Left-click on a stance      │
        │<─────────────────────────────┤
        │                              │
        │     Mage places the mark     │
        │                              ▼
                                   [emoji appears]
```

The sword doesn't cast. It **takes stance** and reveals what's possible.
The mage **executes** — all marks are placed through left-click.

---

## Swordsman (Master) — Right Click

**Right-click to take stance.**

The Master is **deliberate**. Hold right-click to reveal your stance options (the hex grid of available blades). The Swordsman shows *what is possible*.

| Action | What Happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Right-click hold** | Take stance — reveals hex grid of blade options |
| **Left-click on option** | Mage places that stance's emoji on screen |
| **S key** | Open full stance editor (blade inventory, line assignments) |

### Why This Flow?

The Swordsman communicates intent to the Mage:
- Right-click = "I'm taking this stance, here are my options"
- Left-click = Mage receives and executes, placing the mark

The sword never acts alone. It **informs** the mage what stance to express.

---

## Mage (Emissary) — Left Click

**Left-click to cast / place.**

The Emissary is **reactive**. All execution flows through the mage.

| Action | What Happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Left-click tap** | Cast last selected spell immediately |
| **Left-click hold** | View all spells orbiting, choose one |
| **Left-click on stance** | Place stance emoji (when stance menu open) |
| **M key** | Open full mage spell editor (orbit configuration) |

### Why Left Click For Everything?

The left hemisphere executes. Every mark that appears on screen comes through left-click because:
- Placing = action = emissary's domain
- The mage *projects* through boundaries
- The sword *holds* position, mage *acts*

## The Gap Between

The Swordsman reveals. The Mage executes.

Neither can act without the other:
- Right-click alone just shows options (no action)
- Left-click alone casts spells (not stances)
- Together: right-click stance → left-click place

This is the architecture made tactile. The Master attends. The Emissary acts. The coordination happens through **you** — the gap where you live.

## Blade Ring (Swordsman)

The Swordsman's stances come from **forged blades** imported from spellweb.ai.

- 6 stance lines (L1-L6) — one blade per line
- 7th swap slot — always have one blade ready to rotate
- Each blade contributes its facet emoji + hex bit to the stance

**Right-click hold** reveals your blade configuration as a hex grid.
**Left-click** on a stance option places that mark.

See: [chronicle-2026-03-30-witness-sword-bridge.md](./chronicle-2026-03-30-witness-sword-bridge.md)

## Spell Orbit (Mage)

The Mage's spells orbit ready to cast.

- 6 orbit slots (configurable via M key)
- Spells come from grimoire, training, or inscribed stories
- Each spell has an emoji that flies out when cast

**Left-click tap** fires the last spell.
**Left-click hold** shows the orbit, lets you choose.

## The Pattern

1. **Swordsman takes stance** (right-click hold) — reveals options
2. **You see** the hex grid of available stances
3. **Mage places** (left-click) — executes the chosen stance
4. **Mark appears** on screen

Or for spells:

1. **Mage casts** (left-click tap) — immediate action
2. **Or hold** to see orbit, then click to choose

The sword attends. The mage acts. You coordinate.
